The convert-flow how-to doc and its inlined string mirror are out of sync. The drift-catching UAT `test_UAT_FC_REQ-30_system_prompt_includes_howto` (assertion: "the inlined constant matches the canonical .md file byte-for-byte") fails because content was added to `docs/llm-context/reproducing-a-website.md` without being mirrored into `apps/control-app/src/llm-context.ts`.

## Drifted sections (in .md, missing from .ts)

1. **Section 1a — "Execute the entire convert flow in a single turn"** (entire section)
2. **Section 3 — "Wire up the nav"** sub-step (the third numbered item before the modules walk)
3. **Section 6 — second paragraph** — the failure-mid-flow instruction beginning "If `transcribe_site` or `read_transcription_digest` fails mid-flow..."
4. **Section 8 — "Beyond convert — other edit tools"** (entire section listing `duplicate_module`, `set_page_metadata`, `set_nav_pattern`/`set_nav_entries`, `remove_page`/`reorder_pages`)

## Impact

The .ts constant is what actually ships to the LLM at runtime. The .md is documentation. The drift means the AI is currently NOT being told about:

- The single-turn convert-flow contract (the AI may pause mid-convert and let the digest expire).
- The nav-wiring step (multi-page reconstructions may end up with unreachable pages).
- The mid-flow failure-handling instruction.
- The non-convert-time edit tools (duplicate_module, set_page_metadata, etc.).

## Fix

Mirror the drifted sections from the .md into the .ts string. The byte-for-byte assertion will pass once both files have identical content.
